Everyone's Safety Bell  —  Emergency Reporting UX/UI Design

Everyone's Safety Bell is an auxiliary reporting service that can quickly respond to emergencies for vulnerable groups such as single-store operators or single residents in Gyeonggi-do. The service allows reporting through mobile (iOS/Android) and hardware devices, with received reports managed on a PC web screen. This project with Gyeonggi-do involved designing the entire system of the service and UX/UI design for each platform.

Emergency Reporting System for Rapid Response

The reporting system of Safety Bell is designed to respond immediately to emergencies in collaboration with the police. When the report button on the Safety Bell device is pressed, the report signal is transmitted through the registered phone, and the phone sends the emergency report to the police along with the pre-registered report information.

Communication Using Bluetooth (BLE) and Mesh Network

The Safety Bell device is registered and reported through Bluetooth communication (BLE). Even if the primary communication is unstable, the mesh network allows finding nearby other Safety Bells, and the signal is transmitted through the discovered user's phone, ensuring normal reporting.

Mobile App for Reporting and Device Management

The Everyone's Safety Bell mobile app allows users to register the device, check the current status during an actual report, or cancel incorrectly reported contents. It also provides functions to manage the device, such as checking registered device information, communication status, and firmware updates.

Real-Time Response to Received Reports

Emergency reports transmitted through Safety Bell are received not only by the police but also by control centers and response agencies. The received reports can be checked in real-time through the manager screen, enabling rapid response by the person in charge.

Service Status at a Glance with Dashboard

The dashboard on the manager screen provides an overview of the service status and reports. Each area is composed of charts suitable for data characteristics, and the insights gained are utilized to improve the service.

User Management for Service Operation

Registered users for using the Safety Bell service are managed through the user management screen. CS personnel operating the service can check or modify user data, handle various service inquiries through deletion or Excel download, etc.

Project with Government Institutions

This project with Gyeonggi-do was a new challenge with a different nature from typical projects. It involved limited planning/design based on functional requirements and responding to software certifications such as GS certification and web accessibility certification. Managing various stakeholders' different business aspects and coordinating numerous opinions at the center allowed me to learn the experience of responding to various issues.
