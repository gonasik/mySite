Everyone's Safety Bell — Emergency Reporting UX/UI Design

Everyone's Safety Bell is an auxiliary reporting service that can quickly respond to emergencies for vulnerable groups such as single-store operators or single residents in Gyeonggi-do. The service allows reporting through mobile (iOS/Android) and hardware devices, with received reports managed on a PC web screen. This project with Gyeonggi-do involved designing the entire system of the service and UX/UI design for each platform.

Emergency Reporting System for Rapid Response

The Safety Bell reporting system is designed to respond immediately to emergencies in collaboration with the police. When the report button on the Safety Bell device is pressed, the signal is transmitted through the registered phone, which sends the emergency report to the police along with the pre-registered report information.

Communication Using Bluetooth (BLE) and Mesh Network

The Safety Bell device communicates via Bluetooth (BLE). Even if the primary communication is unstable, the mesh network finds nearby Safety Bells, and the signal is transmitted through the discovered user's phone, ensuring the report is sent.

Mobile App for Reporting and Device Management

The Everyone's Safety Bell mobile app allows users to register the device, check the status during a report, or cancel incorrect reports. It also provides functions to manage the device, such as checking registered information, communication status, and firmware updates.

Real-Time Response to Received Reports

Emergency reports sent via Safety Bell are received by the police, control centers, and response agencies. These reports can be checked in real-time through the manager screen, enabling quick response by the responsible parties.

Service Status at a Glance with Dashboard

The manager screen's dashboard provides an overview of the service status and reports. Each area is composed of charts suitable for data characteristics, and the insights gained are used to improve the service.

User Management for Service Operation

Registered users of the Safety Bell service are managed through the user management screen. Customer service personnel can check or modify user data, handle various inquiries, and perform tasks such as deletion or Excel download.

Project with Government Institutions

This project with Gyeonggi-do was a unique challenge different from typical projects. It involved limited planning/design based on functional requirements and responding to software certifications like GS certification and web accessibility certification. Managing various stakeholders' different aspects and coordinating multiple opinions provided valuable experience in addressing diverse issues.
