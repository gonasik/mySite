Swift Student Challenge Winner — WWDC23 Participation

I won the Swift Student Challenge hosted by Apple, and was invited to participate in WWDC23, a conference held at Apple Park in Cupertino, where developers from around the world gather. At the conference, I had the opportunity to experience Apple and the United States, and later shared these experiences at KWDC23.

Design and Development of Moon Phase Observation App (MellowYellows)

For the Swift Student Challenge, I designed and developed a moon observation app using SwiftUI that visually displays the moon phase of the day. The brightness of the stars dims as the moon approaches fullness and brightens as it nears a new moon, capturing the actual appearance of the night sky.

Immersive Animation Effects and Background Music

Smooth and natural animations, such as the rising moon, moving stars, and cloudy skies, created an effect akin to viewing the real night sky. Gentle and peaceful background music enhanced the immersive experience of the app.

Swift Student Challenge Winner

I submitted MellowYellows to the Swift Student Challenge and was fortunate enough to be selected as a winner among students from around the world. As a result, I was invited to attend WWDC23 at Apple’s headquarters in Cupertino and participate in the conference.

WWDC23 Participation at Apple Park

At WWDC23, held at Apple Park, I witnessed the unveiling of new products like Vision Pro and updates to iOS, macOS, watchOS, and more. I also attended presentations on new features in development frameworks like Swift and SwiftUI.

Conversations with Developers and Apple Teams

At the conference, I had the opportunity to meet developers from around the world and members of the Apple team. We discussed newly announced products and technologies, sharing our thoughts and perspectives on various topics.

Speaking at KWDC23

After returning to Korea, I participated as a speaker at KWDC23, a conference for all Apple communities in Korea. I shared my experiences with the Apple ecosystem and services in the US that I encountered at WWDC23.

Growth from New Experiences

From developing my first app to visiting the US and Apple, engaging in conversations with various stakeholders, and presenting at community events, these were all new and refreshing experiences for me. These experiences broadened my perspective in design, development, and other areas, providing significant career inspiration.
